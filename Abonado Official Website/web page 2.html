What is Poverty?

Poverty is a state where individuals or communities lack the financial resources and essentials for a minimum standard of living1. It is characterized by insufficient access to food, clean water, shelter, and healthcare1. Poverty can be absolute, where basic needs for survival are unmet, or relative, where individuals fall below the standard of living in their society2. It is often associated with poor health, low education levels, and limited employment opportunities2. Various factors, including economic instability, lack of education, and social inequality, contribute to the persistence of poverty.
